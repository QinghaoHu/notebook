Ukrainian-Canadian internment:
	By April 1915, feeling against enemy alliens were running so high that the federal government ordered nearly 9000
	people-most of them recent immigrants, including 5000 from Ukraine to be rounded up and taken to twenty-four remote
	internment camp. 

	They were forced to do difficult physical labour such as clearing forests, draining roads and building roads.

Wartime elections act:
	Robert Borden passed the "Wartime Election Act", which allowed nurses and the woman who hand husband/brothers/sons 
	serving in the war to vote in the federal election. 

	Robert Borden promoted to extand the vote to all Canadian Woman if re-elected. He did end up winning, passed 
	the Military Service Act and passed the legislation given all woman the vote in 1920.
